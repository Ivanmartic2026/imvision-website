# IM Vision Design Intelligence

> Research completed 15 July 2026. This is the evidence and strategic rationale behind the IM Vision design system. It is not a mood board and it is not permission to imitate any benchmark.

## 1. Executive conclusion

The strongest premium digital experiences do not share one aesthetic. They share discipline:

1. A single dominant idea is visible within seconds.
2. Type, space, image, and motion all express the same brand character.
3. Product proof appears before decorative complexity.
4. Interactions clarify state and direction; they do not perform for their own sake.
5. Trust is built through specificity: real products, real people, real numbers, real operating detail.
6. Conversion is presented as a natural continuation of the story, not a sales interruption.

IM Vision therefore should not become another black website with cyan glow, floating glass cards, and abstract “technology” copy. Its own territory is **architectural light, visibly engineered**: cinematic installed environments, close technical detail, Nordic restraint, and a distinctive mineral-light palette.

## 2. Method

The study reviewed the current live homepages, information architecture, responsive behaviour, available brand guidance, and content patterns of the named benchmarks. Current Awwwards Sites of the Day, CSS Design Awards winners, and relevant European B2B/industrial experiences were also sampled. Visual observations were made at a 1440 px desktop viewport; structural observations were cross-checked against live page content.

Exact proprietary font names, internal spacing tokens, and unpublished animation curves cannot always be verified from public output. Where a family is not public, the analysis describes its observed typographic class rather than inventing precision.

## 3. Benchmark analyses

### Apple

- **Typography:** SF Pro Display/Text system; large, tightly tracked product headings; very short supporting lines; compact navigation.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Wide modular bands, centered compositions, generous negative space, strong separation between product stories. Grid is simple and almost invisible.
- **Colour and contrast:** Mostly neutral canvases. Colour belongs to the product photography; blue is reserved for action. High text contrast with muted legal copy only at non-critical sizes.
- **Components:** Pill actions, paired “learn/buy” routes, minimal card chrome, persistent global navigation.
- **Motion:** Product-led scroll sequences, slow camera-like transforms, crossfades, and restrained parallax. Hover feedback is quiet and immediate.
- **Imagery and depth:** Controlled studio lighting, immaculate material rendering, large scale, no visual clutter.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Product name → one benefit → two choices. Confidence comes from removing everything that is not essential.
- **Extracted principle:** Make the product the visual event and keep the decision path obvious.

### Porsche

- **Typography:** Proprietary Porsche sans/display character; compressed, confident headlines with supporting utility text.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Full-bleed cinematic stages followed by model/category modules. Strong vertical chapters rather than a continuous card feed.
- **Colour and contrast:** Neutral interface over richly graded automotive film. Brand red is scarce; black/white do most interface work.
- **Components:** Media-overlay controls, solid rectangular actions with moderate radius, model cards led by photography.
- **Motion:** Video first, deliberate reveals, scroll-directed chapters, slow easing. Controls remain functional over immersive media.
- **Imagery and depth:** Architecture, landscape, speed, reflections, and material close-ups. Lighting communicates performance and luxury.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Desire first, model exploration second, configurator/dealer intent third. Technical/legal data stays available without dominating.
- **Extracted principle:** Premium feeling comes from art direction plus operational detail, not from decoration.

### Vercel

- **Typography:** Geist Sans with Geist Mono for technical labels; large low-weight sans display; terse copy.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Precise column grid, strong horizontal rules, dense but ordered product proof. Monochrome surfaces make structure visible.
- **Colour and contrast:** Black, white, grey; rare colour belongs to product demonstrations or status.
- **Components:** Compact pills, hairline borders, terminal/product panels, logo proof rail, highly structured mega-navigation.
- **Motion:** Fast, engineered, state-based. Loading and deployment metaphors become interaction language.
- **Imagery and depth:** Interface demonstrations and generated technical forms rather than lifestyle photography.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Clear platform claim, deploy/demo split, recognizable customer proof immediately visible.
- **Extracted principle:** A technical brand earns trust by making its system legible.

### Stripe

- **Typography:** Proprietary Stripe Sans style with clear grotesk body and monospace where code/data appears. Large editorial headline with controlled line breaks.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Strong vertical grid rails, asymmetric hero, dense ecosystem proof below. Sections layer from value to capabilities to evidence.
- **Colour and contrast:** Deep ink text on pale surfaces with a spectral orange/pink/violet graphic. Colour signals energy without reducing legibility.
- **Components:** Rectangular buttons, subtle radius, outlined secondary actions, product UI cards, customer-logo rails.
- **Motion:** Fluid background fields, product sequences, small directional arrow shifts, medium-speed easing.
- **Imagery and depth:** Abstract brand field plus real software UI. Depth is created through overlap, not generic shadow.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Quantified proof, recognizable customers, outcome-led platform explanation, start/contact routes.
- **Extracted principle:** Complex capability can feel approachable when colour carries emotion and the grid carries order.

### Linear

- **Typography:** Neutral modern grotesk with a precise UI family; small technical figure labels; tightly controlled display scale.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Dark editorial chapters combined with faithful product simulations. Sections use numbered systems and repeated product frames.
- **Colour and contrast:** Near-black, cool greys, low-saturation violet/blue highlights. Borders and light blooms create depth.
- **Components:** Product windows, issue cards, tabs, narrow pills, fine dividers.
- **Motion:** Highly coordinated blur-to-focus, product-state transitions, subtle light movement, fast UI feedback.
- **Imagery and depth:** Product is the imagery. Glow is localized around meaningful focal areas.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Product promise → operating principles → complete workflow → testimonials and scale → CTA.
- **Extracted principle:** Consistency across hundreds of micro-details can become the premium signal itself.

### Nothing

- **Typography:** NDot/pixel display voice paired with utilitarian mono/grotesk text. Typography is deliberately unmistakable.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Campaign-like, experimental compositions balanced by very simple commerce paths.
- **Colour and contrast:** Black/white foundation with assertive campaign colour. Graphic noise and bitmap cues are controlled brand assets.
- **Components:** Hard-edged actions, monospaced utility labels, modular product tiles.
- **Motion:** Playful, sometimes abrupt, with digital distortion and object-led transitions.
- **Imagery and depth:** Transparent product internals, macro detail, youthful editorial photography.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Distinction creates memory; familiar store mechanics preserve usability.
- **Extracted principle:** Recognition requires a repeatable proprietary cue, not just a fashionable palette.

### DJI

- **Typography:** Clean geometric/product sans, large centered product naming, concise benefit taglines.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Full-width product stages with repeated learn/buy actions; product families create predictable chapters.
- **Colour and contrast:** Mostly black/white interface; each product campaign supplies its own environmental colour.
- **Components:** Outline pills, product carousel markers, compact global navigation.
- **Motion:** Hero video, product rotation, slow fades, and media-led slides.
- **Imagery and depth:** Studio renders plus aspirational field footage; precise lens and material close-ups.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Category → product → differentiator → learn/buy. Enterprise links remain accessible but separate.
- **Extracted principle:** Show technical equipment producing an emotional outcome.

### Bang & Olufsen

- **Typography:** Proprietary refined sans with editorial proportions; restrained weight hierarchy.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Gallery-like image fields, generous space, quiet labels, limited concurrent choices.
- **Colour and contrast:** Warm natural neutrals, dark forest/charcoal, metal and textile colours. Avoids “tech blue.”
- **Components:** Understated text links, minimal controls, large editorial modules.
- **Motion:** Slow, tactile, showroom pacing; gentle fades and image pans.
- **Imagery and depth:** Human, architectural, tactile. Materials and domestic context communicate value.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Lifestyle desire supported by product lineage, craftsmanship, and store/service presence.
- **Extracted principle:** Luxury feels warmer and more credible when technology is shown as material craft.

### Tesla

- **Typography:** Custom/brand grotesk; bold centered model labels and compact utility navigation.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Full-viewport panels with repeated bottom actions; extremely shallow hierarchy on the homepage.
- **Colour and contrast:** Photography dominates; interface is black/white with minimal accent.
- **Components:** Broad low-height buttons, transparent/filled pairs, overlay navigation.
- **Motion:** Scroll snapping and background media transitions; little ornamental movement.
- **Imagery and depth:** Cinematic exterior product imagery, clean architectural settings.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Model name → offer/value → order/learn. Minimal friction, but sparse explanation can assume high brand familiarity.
- **Extracted principle:** Repetition of one conversion pattern makes a broad portfolio effortless to scan.

### Rivian

- **Typography:** Custom outdoors-inflected sans family, wide display forms, friendly body text.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Large cinematic chapters with more editorial variety and warmth than Tesla.
- **Colour and contrast:** Earth, sky, warm grey, black, and a controlled golden accent.
- **Components:** Large rounded navigation shell, pill actions, immersive configurator and location modules.
- **Motion:** Vehicle/lifestyle film, 3D configuration, steady scroll reveals.
- **Imagery and depth:** Authentic adventure, people, terrain, and product utility.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Offers and demo drives are visible; safety, charging, software, and mission reduce ownership anxiety.
- **Extracted principle:** A premium technology story becomes human when capability is shown in real life.

### SpaceX

- **Typography:** D-DIN/condensed industrial display character; uppercase utility labels.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Full-screen mission imagery, lower-left headline blocks, direct navigation.
- **Colour and contrast:** Black/white almost exclusively; imagery provides atmosphere.
- **Components:** Square outline action, minimal icon controls, no card-heavy UI.
- **Motion:** Cinematic launch footage and direct vertical transitions; purposeful rather than decorative.
- **Imagery and depth:** Documentary-scale engineering and space imagery.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Mission/event → date/context → watch/learn. Technical achievement itself is proof.
- **Extracted principle:** Monumental scale works when copy remains factual and compact.

### Notion

- **Typography:** Friendly heavy grotesk display with clear UI/body sans; illustrated characters add a second voice.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Centered hero, product screenshot stage, modular benefit blocks, repeated social proof.
- **Colour and contrast:** White/black foundation with bright primary accents tied to illustration and product states.
- **Components:** Conventional buttons, rounded product frames, tabs/cards, illustrated badges.
- **Motion:** Playful object movement, product demonstrations, modest hover shifts.
- **Imagery and depth:** Product UI plus hand-drawn illustration; intentionally approachable rather than luxurious.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Outcome statement → free/demo actions → customer proof → use cases → quantified adoption.
- **Extracted principle:** A complex B2B product can feel easy when the voice is warm and proof is abundant.

### Framer

- **Typography:** Modern neo-grotesk, very large tight display, small product/UI type.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Dark canvas with a massive live product frame. The homepage behaves like a product demonstration.
- **Colour and contrast:** Predominantly black/white; electric blue is functional inside the demonstrated interface.
- **Components:** App-window frames, compact controls, simple pills, large previews.
- **Motion:** Direct-manipulation metaphors, animated canvases, continuous demonstrations; quick UI timing.
- **Imagery and depth:** Software interface at near-real scale, layered with selected customer work.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Promise → try/download → product working → shipped sites → capabilities/performance proof.
- **Extracted principle:** Showing the experience is more persuasive than describing it.

### Figma

- **Typography:** Whyte/Whyte Inktrap brand character paired with clean UI text; large editorial statements.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Intentionally open and asymmetric compositions, framed by familiar product UI patterns.
- **Colour and contrast:** White/black with the multi-colour brand system appearing in controlled graphic blocks.
- **Components:** Bold black actions, outlined sales action, oversized interactive stage.
- **Motion:** Collaborative cursor/object sequences, layered canvas transitions, responsive playful detail.
- **Imagery and depth:** Product surfaces, community artefacts, and real creative output.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Creativity proposition → start action → product breadth → collaboration proof.
- **Extracted principle:** A flexible brand can stay coherent when interaction, type, and colour follow fixed rules.

### Arc Browser

- **Typography:** Expressive editorial serif/display paired with rounded friendly sans; deliberately unlike standard SaaS.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Large colourful campaign bands, centered product demo, visible section-edge treatments.
- **Colour and contrast:** Saturated blue/violet fields, pastel gradients, cream content canvas.
- **Components:** Large tactile CTA, browser-window demonstrations, platform-specific download actions.
- **Motion:** Elastic, playful, highly branded transitions; decorative texture remains secondary to the demo.
- **Imagery and depth:** Product UI with grain, glow, and soft shadow.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Product evolution and security message first, demonstration second, press quote and download next.
- **Extracted principle:** Personality can be a conversion asset when the core task stays obvious.

### Airbnb

- **Typography:** Airbnb Cereal family; friendly rounded forms and strong legibility at compact sizes.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Search is the primary object; content follows in card grids with consistent image ratios.
- **Colour and contrast:** Warm white, dark ink, coral accent, photography-led category colour.
- **Components:** Search capsule, category tabs, image cards, map/list controls, soft radius.
- **Motion:** Fast filters, map feedback, card/image hover, modal transitions.
- **Imagery and depth:** Authentic location/host photography; visual variety is normalized by strict card geometry.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Intent capture first, inventory second, price/rating/location evidence at scan speed.
- **Extracted principle:** A complex catalogue feels simple when the primary intent tool dominates the hierarchy.

### Nike

- **Typography:** Bold condensed Nike display language paired with neutral commerce sans.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Campaign hero, editorial product tiles, strong whitespace, recurring category navigation.
- **Colour and contrast:** Black/white interface; campaign colour and athlete imagery lead.
- **Components:** Compact pill actions, editorial cards, carousels, conventional store controls.
- **Motion:** Athletic cuts, video, carousel momentum, quick hover/image swaps.
- **Imagery and depth:** Real athletes, motion, cultural context, high-contrast art direction.
- **Hierarchy, trust, conversion:** Story and athlete credibility create desire; familiar shop mechanics close the path.
- **Extracted principle:** Energy comes from content direction and pacing, not constant interface animation.

### Awwwards Site of the Day patterns

- **Typography:** Often custom or editorial display faces paired with sober grotesk/mono utility text.
- **Layout and rhythm:** Full-bleed media, unconventional but consistent grids, intentional whitespace, large type.
- **Colour and contrast:** Strong single-world palettes; fewer generic SaaS gradients than expected.
- **Components:** Cursor-aware project cards, sectional navigation, large media controls, experimental menus.
- **Motion:** Scroll choreography, masking, WebGL depth, and page transitions are common; the best entries maintain orientation and reduced-motion alternatives.
- **Trust and conversion:** Portfolio and cultural sites often prioritize memorability over conversion. Their techniques must be filtered before B2B use.
- **Extracted principle:** Borrow commitment and craft, not friction. A current adjacent example is the SOTD-recognized NRG data-centre experience.

### CSS Design Awards winner patterns

- **Typography/layout:** Strong art direction, oversized hierarchy, modular responsive systems, visually evident grids.
- **Interaction:** Hover reveals, cursor states, 3D/WebGL, controlled page transitions, and unusual navigation are recurring.
- **Risk:** Loading cost, accessibility, and unclear action paths can be sacrificed for jury impact.
- **Extracted principle:** Award-level polish belongs in transitions, image treatment, and detail—not in hiding contact and product information.

### Best European B2B technology and industrial experiences

The useful European pattern is represented by brands such as Einride and Floema: a clear industrial mission, authentic operational imagery, restrained typography, system-level product explanation, and evidence of the complete delivery chain. Einride turns autonomous/electric freight into one connected platform story and supports it with live deployments and recognizable partners. Floema combines product taxonomy, manufacturing detail, environmental context, and a full design/build/implement narrative.

- **Typography:** Calm grotesks, occasional mono/condensed engineering label.
- **Layout:** Editorial media paired with disciplined product/service taxonomy.
- **Colour:** Neutral, material, environmentally derived; less default blue.
- **Motion:** Cinematic but slower than consumer SaaS; product/process is the focus.
- **Trust:** Customers, certifications, operating regions, deployment process, engineering detail, named people, and service capability.
- **Conversion:** Contact is persistent, but detailed case studies do most of the persuasion.
- **Extracted principle:** For high-consideration European B2B, evidence of competence converts better than urgency theatre.

## 4. Cross-benchmark design principles

### What consistently works

- One visual thesis, repeated with discipline.
- A limited type system with extreme care in line length and line breaks.
- Large media followed by dense, specific proof.
- Navigation that exposes product/service taxonomy without overwhelming the hero.
- Two-speed pages: slow cinematic moments and fast scanning zones.
- Motion based on physical metaphors: focus, light, camera, material, or interface state.
- Real customer evidence before the final CTA.
- Repeated CTA wording and destination.
- Small technical labels that add precision without turning the whole site into a dashboard.

### What IM Vision must reject

- Electric cyan as the entire identity.
- Neon glow around every interactive element.
- Glassmorphism on ordinary content cards.
- Stock images or fabricated case-study metrics.
- Infinite grids of identical rounded cards.
- Smooth scrolling that harms keyboard, browser-find, or motion-sensitive users.
- Long intro loaders, custom cursors on touch, scroll hijacking, or hidden navigation.
- Unverifiable “best in Europe” claims.
- Award-oriented animation that delays contact, specifications, or service information.

## 5. IM Vision strategic territory

### Position

**IM Vision designs, engineers, installs, and supports architectural light systems that transform physical space.**

The site should feel like entering a working light laboratory inside a Scandinavian architecture studio: calm, exact, tactile, cinematic, and credible.

### Recognition devices

1. **The Light Gate:** image and card frames use a 20–28 px radius with one precision-cut 6 px upper-right corner. It resembles a panel module and becomes a repeatable silhouette.
2. **The Photon Seam:** a one-pixel mineral-to-amber line appears only at boundaries, active states, and key media transitions. It represents calibrated light, not a decorative gradient.
3. **The Calibration Mark:** technical labels use `IM / 01`, crosshair ticks, or measured baselines. These appear sparingly around specifications and process—not on every section.
4. **Dark-to-daylight sequencing:** pages alternate controlled dark “experience” chapters with warm light “evidence” chapters. This mirrors how LED is judged: emotional impact and measured performance.
5. **Media-first proof:** every important claim is paired with an authentic installation, engineer, detail, drawing, or operating metric.

## 6. Content and evidence requirements

Before final production approval, IM Vision needs:

- Original 4K/6K installation footage in landscape and vertical crops.
- Project photography with wide, human-scale, and macro-detail shots.
- Client permissions and correct brand names.
- Verified project locations, dates, pixel pitch, brightness, size, and system type.
- Verified outcomes; remove any statistic that cannot be sourced.
- Real team/warehouse/installation photography.
- Warranty, response-time, certification, and geographic coverage facts.
- Clear distinction between sales, rental, installation, content/control, and service.

The current repository contains Unsplash project imagery and outcome/statistic claims that appear provisional. They must not be presented as client evidence without validation.
